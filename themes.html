Themes

  • Solitude as the function of independence
  • Edna grows as a women throughout The Awakening
  • Woman during the 19th century were very limited in what they were allowed to do socially, they were expected to be mothers formost and often forbidden from expressing themselves
  • Edna longs for independence and the ability to express herself, she initially finds this through swimming and painting
  • However when she tries to express her desire for independence and expression she runs into problems with men, particularly her husband, who believe a woman's role should not include expressing herself
  • Edna realizes that she can not be independent and still connect to the rest of society
  • Self-expression's implications
  • Edna's growth and self expression allows her to learn new languages, find a passion for art, and express her emotions
  • She is very sophisticated and well educated for women of her time, yet she grows more distant from the rest of society
  • Her new found beliefs and skills leave her isolated as she does not seem to connect with many people
  • She takes her own life as her self expression has seem to make her an outcast
