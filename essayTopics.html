Essay Topics

  • Why does Edna become dissatisfied with her marriage?
  • Edna at first seems okay with her marriage to Leonce.
  • The two are not the perfect couple, and he often must spend time away from her for work, however she does not seem to have much of an issue with the arrangement.
  • However when Edna meets Adele while on vacation she begins to learn more about herself as a woman.
  • Adele is interested in the feminist movement and begins to enlighten Edna on the important aspects and desires a woman should have.
  • After spending a lot of time with Adele, Edna realizes that their is a void in her life, and that she cannot be happy living a life with the only focus being on satisfying her husband.
  • Edna realizes that she desires more, and when she meets another man that she feels can provide those things for her, she realizes that her marriage is a sham and she can no longer live pretending that she is happy.
  • What is the significance of the lady in black as well as the two lovers featured in the novel?
  • The lady in black is a widowed woman, and she represents the traditional ideal of a woman in her situation.
  • After her husband died, rather than eventually embarking on a new life for herself, she continues to mourn the death and devote herself to remembering her husband.
  • Edna would be expected to live in much the same way as the woman in black if her husband were to die.
  • Edna sees the woman in black as a pathetic shell of a human who lives primarily through her husband, and the woman in black represents everything that Edna does not want to be.
  • The two lovers on the other hand represent Edna and her relationship with Robert.
  • Edna sees the young couple and thinks about what her relationship with Robert might have been had things gone differently in her life.
  • The two lovers and the lady in black are contrasts of one another and important reminders of where Edna's place in the world is despite any desires of self-sufficiency.
